Igor Dyachenko is a sound and experimental ambient artist, film composer, sound designer, and radio host, currently based in Berlin, Germany.

Conceptualizing his sound practice, one can identify the notion of 'melted reality,' where ambient sound, in constant flux, takes on a hybrid form of physical and abstract medium, interacting with the listener and their environment. Igor's approach to extracting frequencies from diverse sources and shaping them is evident in his work with textured sound, spatial perception, form, and countless layers of events and textures.

In addition to creating albums and performances, Igor develops generative sound systems for architecture, collaborates with fashion brands and multimedia artists, and contributes to soundtracks and sound design for movies, exhibitions and hosts the show Peripherie on Radio Sygma.

His portfolio includes collaborations with Nickelodeon, Salomon, Heliot Emil, Mercedes Benz, Qatar Airlines, and others. His sound has graced films featured in international film festivals and galleries such as The Calvert Journal Film Festival 2021 (Amsterdam), Les Rencontres d’Arles (Paris), Studio Voltaire (London), and Garage Museum (Moscow). His work encompasses a variety of projects exploring and immersing audiences in sonic landscapes, with a focus on European and Russian exhibitions.
